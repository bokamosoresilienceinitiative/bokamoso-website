Who We Are

Where climate science meets creative advocacy

"Bokamoso" means future. We believe securing a resilient future requires local, community-driven action.

The Bokamoso Resilience Initiative is a youth-led, community-centric social enterprise based in Botswana. We bridge the gap between high-level climate policy and grassroots reality.

By equipping children, young people, and vulnerable communities with the tools of climate literacy and disaster preparedness, we ensure that those most affected by environmental changes are the ones leading the solutions.

01

Climate Education & Action

We translate complex climate science into accessible, creative learning experiences for children and youth — making the next generation see themselves as innovators, not victims.

02

Disaster Risk Reduction

We actively step in to fill critical emergency response and awareness gaps in local communities — before disaster strikes, not after.

03

Climate Justice & Policy Advocacy

We ensure youth voices from Botswana aren't just heard at home — they're elevated all the way to global platforms like the UNFCCC COP.

"Young people face the brunt of the accelerating climate crisis — yet they are often left out of the decision-making room. We set out to change that."

— Bokamoso Resilience Initiative, Founded in Gaborone

About Us

Our story, mission & values

BRI operates as a dynamic space where climate science meets creative advocacy. From local workshops to regional climate networks, we build social resilience — giving people the mental, educational, and practical tools to thrive despite environmental challenges.

Our Mission

Empowerment through education, preparedness & advocacy

To empower local communities, particularly youth and children, through creative climate education, community-driven disaster preparedness, and robust policy advocacy.

Our Vision

A climate-resilient Botswana, shaped by its youth

A Botswana where empowered youth actively shape sustainable policies and safeguard their environments — for themselves and for generations to come.

Our Values

01

Youth-Led Innovation

We trust the creativity, energy, and leadership of young people to drive lasting, meaningful change in their communities.

02

Community-Centric Solutions

True resilience starts at the roots — with local knowledge, local needs, and local people setting the agenda.

03

Climate Justice

Everyone deserves a safe, stable environment. We fight for equitable resources and representation for vulnerable communities.

04

Collaboration

We build bridges between civil society, youth networks, and policymakers across Africa — because no one does this alone.

Programs & Pillars

Three pillars of action

Pillar One

Climate Education & The Africa Climate Creatives Fellowship

Standard textbooks aren't enough to inspire climate action. Through creative mediums, storytelling, and local languages, we teach the next generation about environmental stewardship.

Our flagship initiatives engage young minds to see themselves not as victims of climate change, but as innovators and creative advocates for the world they want to inherit.

Africa Climate Creatives Fellowship

Supporting young creative leaders with mentorship, resources, and seed funding to develop community-rooted climate advocacy projects.

01
Pillar Two

Disaster Risk Reduction & Emergency Response

When extreme weather strikes, communities need immediate, localized knowledge to protect themselves. BRI works on the ground to create vital resources that bridge gaps in national emergency responses.

We equip community leaders with frameworks, knowledge, and tools they need — before disaster strikes, not after.

The Botswana Floods Project

Community-focused risk maps, safety guidelines, and awareness campaigns to mitigate flood damage in high-risk zones across Botswana.

02
Pillar Three

Climate Justice & Policy Advocacy

We don't just work in communities; we work in the halls of power. BRI researches and writes evidence-based policy briefs that reflect the lived realities of people in Botswana.

We train youth advocates to navigate high-level political spaces — ensuring meaningful participation in national frameworks and global climate summits, including the UNFCCC COP.

Youth Policy Briefs

Actionable, evidence-based documents developed with and by young people — designed to influence local, regional, and international climate decision-making.
